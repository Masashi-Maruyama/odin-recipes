Beef Stew

Description

The key to beef stew is red wine. Although the alcohol content is lost during the stewing process, it adds flavor and richness, giving the stew a much deeper taste.

Cut vegetables such as potatoes and carrots into large chunks for a hearty, satisfying flavor that is perfect for the volume of beef!

Ingredients

(4-6 servings)

  • Beef (for stew) __400g
  • Potatoes __ 3-4 pieces
  • carrot __ 2 to 3 carrots
  • Onion (large) __ 1 onion
  • Granulated soup stock __ 1 1/2 teaspoons
  • Hot water __ 1 liter
  • salt __ a pinch of salt
  • pinch of coarsely ground black pepper __ a pinch
  • bay leaves __ 2 leaves

Group of A

  • Red wine __ 1 cup
  • butter __ 3 tablespoons
  • Worcestershire sauce __ 3/4 cup
  • Ketchup __ 6 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Dissolve granulated soup stock in hot water to make soup.
  2. Sprinkle the beef with salt and coarsely ground black pepper.
  3. Heat a large frying pan (or pot) over medium-high heat, add the beef without oil and brown it quickly, add the broth and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, remove the scum and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Peel potatoes, cut into large chunks, and rinse in cold water. Peel carrots and cut into 1.5 cm thick half-moon slices. Cut onion into bite-sized pieces.
  5. Add the potatoes and carrots to the pan, cover, and cook over low-medium heat for another 30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add the onions, season with A, and cook for another 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    * When adding A, you may adjust the amount of Worcestershire sauce or ketchup to taste.
